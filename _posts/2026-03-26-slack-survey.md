Next week, the Slack will conduct the first survey for highly active users. The Census will run monthly, and feature a mix of recurring questions and one-off investigations.

The Census is going to a random sample of the most active users to avoid survey fatigue in the future. This time, that means anyone who was active for at least 20 out of the past 30 days at time of sample. Subjects will receive a DM from @Hack Club Census.

This year we've conducted a monthly survey of newly-joined Slack users covering their NPS and written feedback on the Slack. New users naturally differ from the most active users, so surveying a distinct population will reveal different information. New users' experience is helpful to understand the Slack's retention rate, which is a decisive factor in what happens to the Slack.

The questions on the census are as follows:

1. Have you recommended the Slack to a friend?
2. Why or why not?
3. Why do you spend time on the Slack?
4. How much of your time do you spend in public vs private channels?
5. How would you rate the Slack's public channels?
6. How would you rate the Slack's private channels?
7. Do you feel like you are growing into the person you want to be by spending time on the Slack?
8. What is one question you wish I had asked you? What is the answer to that question?
