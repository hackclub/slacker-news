title: Debate to be Held on Moderation Transparency
---
There will be a debate on Sunday, March 22 at 10:15 AM EDT, in #eps-conduit, on the subject of moderation transparency in the Slack.

Right now, when a user is banned or shushed, or when a thread is locked or deleted, it appears in #community-logs. In the case of a permanent ban, the Fire Department provides a basic reason why, specifying which part of the Code of Conduct was violated.

This system is a periodic source of controversy, and attracts calls for change in opposite directions. Some have argued that reasons should be given for all penalties, or that the Fire Deparment should share details of cases. Others (including me) have argued that #community-logs should be discontinued, or made read-only. Users have attempted to create crowdsourced alternatives that provide more information than #community-logs.

The time is right to make progress on this disagreement. HQ is developing a new conduct reporting system that spans all departments within Hack Club, and inevitably, decisions will have to be made about how much of that information is public.

There will be time set aside for audience questions.
