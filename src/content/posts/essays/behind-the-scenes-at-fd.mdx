Have you ever wondered what the Fire Department is up to? Most of our work is kind of behind the scenes and the community doesn't really get to know what happens. So I'm here to give you a sneak peek at what we've been doing over the past 3 months and better explain what that looks like.

In the span of the past 3 months:

- We got about 1000 Shroud reports, which goes to about 11/day.
- We've also had about 330 conduct cases resolved and logged through LYLA (our moderation logging system), which goes to a little more than 3 cases being filed each day.

Out of 330 cases the split of infractions looks like this:

| Infraction                             |   Count |
| -------------------------------------- | ------: |
| NSFW                                   |      54 |
| Expressing insulting/demeaning remarks |      54 |
| Advertising or recruiting              |      39 |
| Expressing hateful remarks             |      33 |
| Spam                                   |      33 |

And when it comes to the actions we took, the split looks like this:

You might be wondering how we got 1000 Shroud reports but only 330 ended up becoming resolved conduct cases in LYLA. Some reports are duplicates submitted by different people, some others don't have enough context or information for us to work off of and some are about stuff that doesn't break the Code of Conduct. Every report we get is looked at even if the outcome ends up being no action taken by us. In some rare cases we don't log actions we take. This is specific to informal DM reminders and age-related cases like those involving adults.

When we get a report, we usually need to get context and evidence (if it isn't already given), look at previous history and sometimes discuss amongst ourselves before a decision is made, especially if a case is complex. Some reports can be reviewed in a few minutes, while others can take much longer which again is dependent on the complexity.

One of the biggest changes we've seen over the past three months has been an increase in the volume of reports we get due all the new people joining from Stardance. We had about 800 Shroud reports between January and April while LYLA cases filed remained just about the same. As more people join the community, it becomes more active so the number of reports is bound to increase. We got a new batch of firefighters that came in and they have been doing a phenomenal job at making sure these reports are handled well. Over the past week alone we have had a median case closure time of about an hour which is incredible from our previous 3 hour case closure time before the new batch joined.

Bans and shushes are the moderation actions that everyone usually sees (cough… <SlackChannel id="community-logs" />), but as you can tell around half of all conduct cases over the past three months were resolved with warnings while bans and shushes make up a much smaller portion. Because warnings happen in private it is easy to assume moderation is mostly about banning or shushing people. In reality a lot of our time is spent helping people who break the Code of Conduct understand what they did wrong and giving them the chance to be better.

While putting these numbers together we realised what we do is pretty much invisible. So we talked about this in the Fire Dept and we think it's important for the community to have a better understanding of what we are doing. So over the coming weeks, we'll be adding moderation statistics like the ones here to <SlackChannel id="community-logs" />. For now we won't be removing individual logs, but we hope this new format helps answer your questions about what we do while being as transparent as we can and giving everyone a better idea of how we work to keep Hack Club a welcoming and enjoyable space for everyone!
