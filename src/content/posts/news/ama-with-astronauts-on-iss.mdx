Hack Club’s programs reach people all over the world, but nobody in space. We are officially fixing this oversight. On September 1, our AMA series will cross the Kármán line to reach the crew of the International Space Station.

What would you ask an astronaut? NASA gives this advice:

- Unique questions.
- Career-focused questions.
- Research-based questions.
- Work-related questions.
- Specific skills questions.
- We get lots of questions that are repetitive; try to think outside the box.

Want to ask a question? Join the [Stardance Challenge](https://stardance.hackclub.com/), our program where you make cool STEM projects and get free STEM prizes. The shop is selling 12 questions for the AMA, along with everything from macbooks to hot chocolate, and once-in-a-lifetime experiences like these! Your questions and the astronauts’ answers will be presented on the [Learn with NASA](https://www.youtube.com/@LearnWithNASA) youtube channel. Our usual format for terrestrial guests will have to change; questions will be pre-recorded and must be submitted to NASA for screening no later than August 1.

The ISS orbits the Earth at 76.6 kilometers per second. It is arguably the most expensive object ever created. It is also a reminder that when we let go of fear and conflict, we can build things together that change the world.

Start thinking of questions.
