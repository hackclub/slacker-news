I have been collecting this data since January. I also include prose feedback questions. In general, the themes of positive and negative comments have remained the same all year.

**Positive Feedback**
- Great community and people
- Useful resources
- Great place to build things
- YSWS and hackathons are amazing

**Negative Feedback**
- Navigating the channels is overwhelming
- I wish there were a channel for X. (In general, this channel already exists, meaning they couldn't find it)
- It's hard to connect with people
    - It's hard to connect with people who speak my (non-English) language
    - It's hard to connect with people from my country/region
- Once I'm here, it's not clear what I'm supposed to *do*

**What We've Tried**
I'm constantly trying to improve the new user experience. Lucky for me, you all step up to help. Here are some of the things we've done:
- slack.hackclub.com
- readme.hackclub.com
- #help
- #welcome-to-hack-club
- Making new users full members instead of multi-channel guests
- Converting all legacy multi-channel guests to full members
- Fun events for newly-joined people

Even though we have not "fixed the Slack", I do think these efforts have made a difference. For example, people who say they joined through slack.hackclub.com have the second-highest NPS of any join method, *and* their NPS has increased over time. My theory is that new user NPS is currently dominated by factors outside of our control. But this does not mean we can't make big improvements. We need to invent things that make a huge positive difference for new users. When we do, these things will come to be the dominant factors in NPS.
