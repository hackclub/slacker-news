Most community made events start in <SlackChannel id="ysws" /> on Slack. That first step has changed slightly a few weeks ago.

But first, what is a You Ship We Ship (YSWS) programme? YSWS's are events Hack Club runs where you 'ship' a project and Hack Club ships something in return. This could be anything from making a website and getting $5 to buy boba or spending 40 hours making CAD projects to get a 3D printer.

Slack is used to promote your YSWS idea. For example, when I was searching for a sponsor for my YSWS Woof I received lots of engagement from the community through upvotes and because I posted regularly my draft's advert in <SlackChannel id="ysws" />. Before the new rule, you could post multiple times a week - now you are limited to once a week. This is to clean up the channel and make sure that all YSWS's can be viewed easily and make it easier for people to find events they are interested in. Also, you must not have a fillout embedded in your post as it takes up too much space.

There is an exciting change coming in this new system as well. Every week an update on all the current YSWS Drafts and current programs will be posted in <SlackChannel id="ysws-bulletin" />. This will declutter <SlackChannel id="ysws" /> and provide an easy-to-read rundown on current programs. To add your YSWS to the queue just fill out [this form](http://forms.hackclub.com/roundup).

Just as I was finishing writing this article a poll was posted in <SlackChannel id="ysws" /> asking about the community's overall opinion on the changes and current problems. As of Tuesday the 21st, people who liked the changes outnumbered people who disliked the changes 10 to 1. A large group was also neutral or undecided.

This shows that while we don't have all the answers, we're headed in the right direction. If you have an idea for how to improve <SlackChannel id="ysws" /> or an idea for an event then head over to the slack channel or take a look at the new bulletin.
