One of the complex challenges of any large community is the signal-to-noise ratio. As Hack Club has grown, so too has the complexity of maintaining a space that is productive, welcoming, and above all else, positive! Public channels are the town squares of our Slack, and like any town square, they require stewardship and delicate care. After months of careful deliberation, deep reflection on the nature of community, and several very long meetings, we are proud to announce a significant evolution in how we approach that stewardship: a global automod rollout for @Prometheus!

The philosopher [John Rawls](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Rawls) once argued that a just society requires institutions that protect its members from harm, even harm they don't yet know they're experiencing. We took that to heart. We've noticed low-quality posts, off-topic tangents, and certain recurring conversational patterns quietly eroding the quality of our shared spaces, so we're taking proactive steps rather than reacting. Waiting for harm to occur and cause a <SlackChannel id="meta" /> thread before addressing it has proven to be ineffective before, so we are trying something new.

@Prometheus will be present in most public channels, watching over public spaces and keeping them pro Hack Club. Think of it less as surveillance and more as ambient guidance, a gentle, ever-present hand on the shoulder of conversations to help keep it productive and forward the organization.

Powered by our [new in-house AI, Mole](https://hackclub.slack.com/archives/C0266FRGT/p1775059206361919), Prometheus now supports extensive pattern analysis across message data (fully anonymized, I think), to identify phrases that have historically been used to derail productive conversations, introduce unnecessary friction, or generally bring vibes down. These terms have been added to the automod, and we will continue expanding this list as the community evolves and as we learn more about the specific linguistic patterns that threaten positivity. Prometheus will now automatically log these terms, but don't worry, it won't be intrusive. Most of the time, just knowing it's watching will be enough.

We believe this change reflects a mature, considered approach to community governance. We hope you welcome this change as we work to make your Slack experience better, more positive, and more aligned with our mission. We also warmly ask that you invite @Prometheus to your private channels as well, so we can extend this culture of proactive positivity into every corner of the community. The more ground we can cover, the better we can serve you.
