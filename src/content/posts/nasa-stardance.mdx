You may have noticed us dropping NASA-related hints lately. This is not a coincidence. This summer, Hack Club and NASA are joining forces to bring you something new: Stardance.

A few months ago, Zach and Christina had a meeting with Jared Isaacman, the current administrator of NASA. They talked about how space exploration requires a new generation of engineers, but too many teenagers are just consumers of technology and don’t know how to be makers. Hack Club is on a mission to change that. You already know this part. But since then, we’ve been working with NASA behind the scenes, and now we’re ready to tell you that this thing is really happening. 

Stardance is coming this summer and we’re so excited to see what you do with it. But before we open the doors and let you in, we have to build it. We’re announcing now because we will be inviting community members to help us make Stardance, without tripping over the need to keep secrets.

For the moment, the existence of Stardance is just about all we are prepared to announce, but more is coming from here on out. Join <SlackChannel id="stardance-construction" /> (<SlackChannel id="shhhhhh-contruction" /> at time of writing) and you’ll be the first to know.

Last week, our offices stayed open late because the staff were watching the Artemis launch. I know a lot of you were watching it too. I’ve been fascinated by space for as long as I can remember, but I especially remember playing Kerbal Space Program. I always assumed that subjects like orbital mechanics would just be permanent mysteries, that I would never be smart enough for them. I’ll never forget how I felt to learn that actually, I had the power to understand them.

Stardance isn’t only about space. It is about the larger idea that there is still magic left to discover, and that you–yes, you–can be part of it, can go out and do something life-changing, that you never thought was possible for you.
