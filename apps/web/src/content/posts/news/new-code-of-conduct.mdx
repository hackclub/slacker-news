We've drafted a new Code of Conduct for Hack Club. This new code is not yet in effect. You can see it below, and discuss it [here](https://hackclub.slack.com/archives/C01D7AHKMPF/p1778255464795189). 

**Changes**

1. "Keep it appropriate for the context" has been added as a rule. This formalizes pre-existing practice like forbidding discussion of graphic political violence out side of #politics.
2. The code has been significantly shortened.
3. The code has been organized around four fundamental principles to make it easier to follow the spirit of the rules.

**Why Change it?**

The new code is not meant to be a different set of rules. It is meant to be the same rules, but simpler and easier for new Hack Clubbers to follow.

The Code of Conduct applies in all Hack Club spaces. This means, for example, that it applies at a Hackathon. When people go to Campfire, they are supposed to know and follow the CoC. The current code has information that 
doesn't apply to them. We have to be fair and realistic about how many rules we are asking new people to take on.

**Clarity vs Specificity**

The new code is meant to be clear and simple. But that's in direct conflict with having the code be detailed and specific. People don't want to cross the line, and don't want to be forced to guess where the line is, so they want the line drawn clearly. This is important.

The Slack has more rules than anything else in Hack Club, because it's older and more complicated than other Hack Club programs. This results in a lot of rules and details that are only relevant to the Slack. Those rules need to live somewhere, but they shouldn't be part of the slide we present to a first-time hacker attending a Horizons meetup.

So we will create a separate Slack Rules page, which provides detail for the people who need it, without imposing on the people who don't.

**Public Comment**

I am putting this out before it goes into effect so that we have a chance to talk about it. I said this is not supposed to be a change to the substance of the rules. If you feel that it is, I would like to hear about it.

For reference, the current code is [here](https://hackclub.com/conduct). The new code is below.

## The Whole Point

Most online spaces are somewhere between a gas station and a nuclear waste dump. At best, people come, take what they need, and leave. At worst, people poison each other, themselves, and everything around them.

It doesn't have to be like this. An online community can be a place where people respect you, and care what happens to you. It can be the best community of your life. That is what we are doing at Hack Club. When you join us, you are committing to help us make that kind of community. This is really hard, but totally worth it. Your job is to follow these principles:

1. Treat everyone with respect and kindness
2. Don't be destructive or inflammatory
3. Make conflict productive
4. Be Good, not just Not Bad

These principles are always good, but not always clear, so click on each one to understand how we apply it in practice. Those are the rules, but the point of the rules is the principles. No set of rules can perfectly capture the right thing to do in every situation. We will always hold you, and ourselves, to these values.

## Where the Code of Conduct Applies

- Our Slack
- Our GitHub organization and repositories
- Clubs
- Events (in-person or online) and affiliated programs
- Student-run organizations on HCB

The code of conduct applies to everyone who participates in these spaces, including Hack Clubbers, volunteers, staff, and alumni.

We may still take action when Hack Clubbers break the code of conduct while interacting with each other in non-Hack Club spaces.

## Treat everyone with respect and kindness

1. Be friendly and welcoming
2. Do not say anything insulting, demeaning, hateful, or threatening
3. Do not discriminate against people in your words or actions
4. Do not harass or bully people
   1. If someone asks you to leave them alone, you must leave them alone, even if you have friendly intentions
5. Do not make unwelcome romantic or sexual advances
   1. If you don't know whether advances will be welcome, don't make them

## Don't be destructive or inflammatory

1. Don't post things that degrade the quality of our shared spaces:
   1. Sexual content
   2. Spam
      1. Almost anything becomes spam if you do it too much
   3. Ads without HQ permission
2. Keep it appropriate for the context. Things might be acceptable in one context, but break the Code of Conduct in a different context. For example:
   1. Jokes or memes that derail a serious discussion
   2. Sharing or asking for personal information with people who don't want to hear or give you personal information
   3. Discussing sensitive and potentially uncomfortable topics around people who don't want to talk about them, or are too young to talk about them
3. Do not attempt to defraud Hack Club

## Make conflict productive

1. When someone disagrees with you, first try to understand what they think and why
2. Do not escalate, even when provoked
3. Do not rally sentiment against others
4. Assume good faith
5. Be patient
6. Think about how your words will be interpreted. Productive communication requires effort
7. Take a moment to think before you speak in heated or fast-moving conversations
8. Sometimes, it's better to simply refrain from saying anything at all
9. If you need help, reach out to the moderation team

## Be Good, not just Not Bad

The wrong way to do Hack Club is to ask "what is the minimum needed to stay within the rules?".

The right way to do Hack Club is to ask "what would a good person do in this situation?" and then do that, even if you don't want to, and even if you think the rules don't require it.

We all fall short sometimes. When it happens to you, don't try to excuse it to yourself or others by saying "it's not against the rules".
